Kane 1524

What a transformation! Those of you who have been watching Kane since he first arrived at the shelter several months ago are not going to BELIEVE this is the same dog.

Kane, a beautiful black Labrador/shepherd mix born 5/31/2020, came in as an untrained, undisciplined, big (70-pound) dog with a great heart but no manners whatsoever - a full grown year-old puppy without a clue.

We brought in a trainer, worked with Kane, the staff, and the volunteers to find the best ways to motivate and communicate with Kane, and this boy has completely changed how people react to him. In the early days, seeing Kane running toward you would elicit an "oh, no!" and you'd need to brace yourself for his enthusiastic leap into your arms (he never did understand why no one could catch him). Now, everyone says "YAY, it's Kane, what a good boy!"

Kane is eager to please, he is potty trained, waits patiently for his turn to go outside and returns happily for his treat (which he receives gently, such a good boy). While he loves some good exercise and playing outside, you can often find Kane relaxing on the couch, snuggled up with a volunteer. He soaks up every little bit of love he can! Though he can jump up occasionally in his excitement and joy, he is easily redirected. Kane knows how to sit and stay. He catches onto routine quickly and is a smart boy ready to learn more commands and tricks!

Kane does have a high prey drive so should not be placed with cats, poultry, or other small "living squeaky toys." He does enjoy playing with similarly sized friendly dogs who are up for some energetic romping and wrestling; if you have a big, playful dog at home, we recommend coming to Republic to let your dog and Kane meet prior to adoption, as dogs - like people - can sometimes be selective about their friends. Kane is a very happy, loving boy ready to be your next best friend!

The video shows what a good boy Kane is - and he's not even 2 years old yet, so has many terrific years to spend with you!
