Bourne 1605

Bourne is a 50-pound boisterous pup, born 7/17/21. Based on appearance, he may have some heeler, maybe some hound, and a whole lot of who-knows-what in the mix - what we lovingly call a LeMutte pup! They are the best kind, each one is totally unique.

He was transferred in from a neighboring shelter, and is the quintessential diamond-in-the-rough pup. He has a wonderful, loving heart and a fun character. Bourne arrived with no real training, other than appearing to be housetrained. His enthusiasm for every kind word, treat, or moment of affection spills over into the typical overexcited pup behaviors of jumping, mouthing, nose-booping. He is extremely treat motivated, and thanks to the success the staff and volunteers had in turning around these overexcitement issues in Kane, Bourne is now beginning an in-shelter behavior modification plan, under which positive behaviors like remaining calm and sitting for treats are rewarded while unwanted behaviors are ignored or shunned (never punished - we advocate for positive training methods, teach the dog what behavior you want, never punish for unwanted behavior). He is a smart cookie, and will learn quickly and carry on learning once in his new family.

Bourne would love an active family with some experience with positive dog training (or the willingness to enroll in training classes with him - remember, training is 99% about training the humans in how to communicate with the dog!). Due to his rambunctious nature, we recommend no toddlers (aka, "human bowling pins" for an enthusiastic 50-pound doggo). Older, sturdy, patient kids who can understand and follow training tips would be great. We have not cat-tested Bourne yet, and based on his excitability, it's pretty safe to assume he would chase a cat that runs. What he would think about a cat that just sits there, we don't know - but if you have a dog-savvy cat, we will be happy to test Bourne out with one of our shelter "cat potatoes" (non-dog-reactive cats) to see how he does. He seems to enjoy the company of other dogs and might benefit from having a similarly sized canine companion; otherwise, he'd be quite happy as an only dog, as long as he had food, shelter, love, and clear training instructions. Dogs like Bourne thrive when they understand what is expected of them; we could easily see Bourne picking up activities like agility, search and rescue, or just hiking.
